ERA

"a long and distinct period of history." Yes Google, I don't live through multiple Eras but this term is just too cool to give up. "Era" is what I use to describe a specific time frame where significant / life changing events happened.

Minecraft ERA (Primary Education)

Everyone was a crack head addicted to Minecraft, but little to none could afford it, and instead used cracked launchers. I once told myself I would play Minecraft eternally (Obviously that was a lie xd)

GTA ERA (Primary Education)

Minecraft was still a popular game but a classmate decidedly torrented (These kids ahead of the game) GTA, and it spreaded like wildfire (Via USB). Those who got the copy of the game were considered respected individuals, while others have to do something in exchange of what can be copied from a USB in under 3 mins (USB 2.0 was slow).

Blackshot ERA (Primary / Secondary Education)

One of the first multiplayer FPS games I've ever played. It was fun not because the game was good (P2W FPS Games suck.), but due to the interactions with other fellow players.

TF2 ERA (Secondary Education)

Many became addicted to Team Fortress 2, an FPS Game developed by Valve. It was fun and had many classes to choose from, my favourite being Sniper, which I clocked 1000 + hours into. The Game has a "premium status" attained by making any form of in-game purchase, but due to our limited means of purchasing virtual goods back then, anyone with the "premium status" were described as "Being bestowed by the gods".

CS:GO ERA (Secondary Education)

Nothing lasts forever, and Games ain't an exception. TF2 players moved to CS:GO because it was a "More competitive game". CS:GO was a paid game back then; as an individual who frown upon paid games, I did not pick CS:GO up.

Clash Royale ERA (Secondary Education)

Clash Royale exploded with fame, and the very fact that its a mobile game meant that a great majority of people played it. It was a fun game, and I enjoyed it despite being absolute garbage at it. Begged my parents for a $30 Gift Card for my birthday just to purchase something in-game.

Overwatch ERA (Secondary Education)

Another game which exploded with fame. But it was a costly game (60 Bucks back then) so it was a definite no for me.

Mobile Legends ERA (Tertiary Education)

Now, with the means to purchase online goods (Thanks to credit card), this is one of the games I spent a fortune on. Until...
